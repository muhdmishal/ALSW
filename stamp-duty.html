Stamp Duty Land Tax (SDLT) changes

4th December 2014

Significant changes to the level of SDLT payable on the purchase of a property have been introduced by George Osborne in his Autumn Statement. Many believe that it is a much fairer system as it is now applied at different rates depending upon the portion of the purchase price that falls into each rate band, rather than as a single percentage of the overall purchase price. George Osborne believes that as many as 98% of homebuyers will benefit.

Those who have already exchanged contracts and who will be completing today or later, can chose whether they want to pay the Tax in accordance with the old rules of the new rules.

New Rates

Residential properties

Purchase price of property SDLT (percentage of portion of purchase price)
£0 - £125,000 0%
£125,001 - £250,000 2%
£250,001 - £925,000 5%
£925,001 - £1.5 million 10%
Over £1.5 million 12%

Corporate bodies

SDLT is charged at 15% on residential dwellings costing more than £500,000 bought by bodies such as companies and collective investment schemes, unless the property is to be used for a property rental business, a property development or resale trade or for providing admission to visitors on a commercial basis, in which case you pay SDLT based on the new rates and bands.

Residential leases

If your residential lease is for more than £125,000, you will pay 1% SDLT on the amount above the £125,000 threshold.

Non-residential and mixed-use properties

Purchase price/lease premium or transfer value SDLT (percentage of portion of purchase price)
Up to £150,000 - annual rent less than £1,000 0%
Up to £150,000 - annual rent £1,000 or more 1%
Over £150,000 to £250,000 1%
Over £250,000 to £500,000 3%
Over £500,000 4%
